\section{Decomposition}

For $A\in\mathbb{R}^{m\times n}$ and $k=\min(m,n)$, a reduced SVD is
\[
  A=U\Sigma V^\top,
  \quad U\in\mathbb{R}^{m\times k},
  \quad \Sigma\in\mathbb{R}^{k\times k},
  \quad V^\top\in\mathbb{R}^{k\times n}.
\]
The singular triplets satisfy
\[
  Av_i=\sigma_i u_i,
  \qquad A^\top A=V\Sigma^2V^\top,
  \qquad AA^\top=U\Sigma^2U^\top.
\]

\section{Rank-One Expansion and Truncation}

The decomposition can be written
\[
  A=\sum_{i=1}^{k}\sigma_i u_i v_i^\top.
\]
Keeping the first $r$ terms gives
\[
  A_r=U_{:r}\Sigma_{:r,:r}V_{:r}^\top.
\]
The accuracy and storage consequences of truncation depend on the stated norm,
matrix dimensions, spectrum, and downstream use.

\section{Centered Data and PCA}

For a centered data matrix $X_c=U\Sigma V^\top$, the first $r$ principal scores are
\[
  X_cV_{:r}=U_{:r}\Sigma_{:r,:r}.
\]
