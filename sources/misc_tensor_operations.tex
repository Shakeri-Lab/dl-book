\section{Operation Families}

Elementwise multiplication and matrix multiplication are different contracts:
\[
  C_{ij}=A_{ij}B_{ij},
  \qquad
  (AB)_{ij}=\sum_k A_{ik}B_{kj}.
\]
A reduction removes named axes unless a singleton dimension is retained. Broadcasting
aligns shapes from the trailing axes and permits equal sizes or a singleton size.

\section{Indexed Contractions}

For attention-style arrays
$Q\in\mathbb{R}^{B\times H\times T\times D}$ and
$K\in\mathbb{R}^{B\times H\times S\times D}$, the score contraction is
\[
  R_{bhts}=\sum_d Q_{bhtd}K_{bhsd}.
\]

\section{Shape and Layout Operations}

Reshaping, transposing, permuting, indexing, and masking may change axis meanings,
strides, storage sharing, or all three. A correct shape alone does not establish a
correct semantic contract.
